
Wislandic uses base 12, see [[Base 12 (Dozenal)]] for a refresher

Wislandic society has many native units for things, while global, SI units are not *unfamiliar* to wislanders broadly, they are mostly used in formal, academic contexts. For most things, native units are used.

## Length

The basic unit of length is the *Omet*, the length of Wisleidenn's hand. Many cultures have created units of measurements based on shared cultural references such as barley corns or rulers' body parts. But every person has different sized body parts, as is every barley corn unique. This is where the Lady of Wis once again serves as a shared candle in the darkness of the world. For she has been around since the first generation, and will continue to remain until the end of days. 

Further more, the *Omet* can be subdivided into 12ths, each fraction being 1 *Kleyu* or claw, as it is about the length of a wislander's claw. For the smallest of things, there is the *Sprek*. 1 Sprek is 1/12th of a Kleyu.

Likewise, 12 *Omette* is 1 *Hrak*, or wingspan. As it is roughly the span of Kon Drakki's wings in her common form.

For longer distances, such as that between villages and towns, There is the *Fardem* and *Alfard*. The *Fardem* is *Tsae Hrakke*, or 1728 (1000 base 12) Hraks. Originally, Fardem was the word for a journey or trip, but was analogized to be a unit of distance one would travel. However, it eventually came to be used solely as a unit, with the general case being supplanted by *Farth*.

The *Alfard*, or "Half Fardem", is half the length of a Fardem. (equivalent to 864 base 10/600 base 12 Hraks) This unit was originally for measuring areas of land. The idea being, breaking one Fardem in half and making each half length the side of a square. And large plots of land are still measured in *Alfardde* today. But as contact with the outside world became more prevalent and official trading relationships were established, the Alfard was a more familiar distance to the outside world, as such, many texts used *Alfarda* for the half Fardem as a unit of length in their translations. Coincidentally, Half a Fardem is very close to a "nautical mile", only off by ~3%.
