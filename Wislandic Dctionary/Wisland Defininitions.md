# Wisland
*Wisland's, Land of Wis, Land of Wis'*

The fictional landmass and country that is home to the Lady of Wis and her children.

---
# Wislandic
*Wislandic's*

1. (n.) The language spoken and written in Wisland, and the native tongue of most Wislanders. *"The message was written in __Wislandic__".*
2. (adj.) An adjective denoting something relating to Wisland. *"__Wislandic__ food is delicious!"*
---
# Wislander
*Wislanders, Wislander's, Wislanders', wislander, wislanders, wislander's, wislanders'*

1. (n.) A wislander is anyone whom lives in or hails from Wisland. While those from outside Wisland often conflate the ethnicity and residency of Wislanders, strictly speaking a Wislander is *only* one who lives in Wisland. Whether they are a Child of Wis or human.
---
# Child of Wis
*Children of Wis, Children of Wis', Child of Wis', Wistankoe, Wistankoelh*

1. (n.) A translation of the Wislandic word *Wistankoe*, which is used as a term for those who worship the Lady of Wis, and have received her Grace. Those who have, bare The Lady's mark on the back of their right hand. Carries more of an ethnic connotation, but on paper includes all whom take a covenant with The Lady of Wis.
---
# Outlands
*Outland, outlands, outland*

1. (n.) A translation of the Wislandic word *Autla*, which is any land that is outside the Wisland. Despite being singular in form, it carries a general collective connotation. Additional clarification is used when speaking about a specific place outside of Wisland
---
# Outlander
*Outlanders, Outlander's, Outlanders', outlander, outlanders, outlander's, outlanders'*

1. (n.) A translation of the Wislandic word *Autlaki*. An outlander is anyone from the outlands. Not a wislander. Can be used as a pejorative depending on context.
---
